-Where in the world is Egypt?
Ancient Egypt was located on the northern coast of Africa along the Mediterranean sea and mostly along the Nile river. At times the Egyptians spread their control along the Mediterranean Sea but mostly they controlled along the Nile River.
-How did Ancient Egypt's location help it grow?
Egypt has deserts to the east and west, the Mediterranean Sea to the north and high mountains to the south.Egypt was well situated, with natural barriers for defense.
-Were the ancient Egyptians isolated from other cultures?
Egypt was not isolated. Sailing along the Mediterranean was not too hard so they had contact with many other peoples.
-How did the Nile River help Egypt grow?
The Nile was the most important feature for ancient Egypt. The river provided food (fish, birds, animals lived in or along it), building supplies (papyrus, trees and clay), easy transportation of people and goods, and every year the Nile flooded bringing rich soil down the river to cover the land to help farmers grow food. These were all "gifts from the Nile".
-Wouldn't living along just one river restrict a civilization?
The Nile is one of the world's longest rivers. Over 4,000 miles long and flowing south to north, the Nile River allowed the Egyptians to stay connected and build a culture and a civilization.
-Was ancient Egypt a country or a collection of city-states?
Ancient Egypt started out as two countries, but was united into one country in ancient times by the cleverness of King Menes and his two colored hat.
-What was the ancient Egyptian government like?
Egyptian government was Pharaoh. Pharaoh owned everything including the land, the livestock, even the house you lived in, the food you ate, and the clothes you wore.
-How did Pharaoh run ancient Egypt by himself?
Pharaoh had many people to run the country on a daily basis including tax collectors, judges, police, and soldiers.The most important person beside Pharaoh was the Vizier. The Vizier oversaw everyone else and reported to Pharaoh every day.
-In ancient Egypt, were pharaohs the same as kings?
Yes. The rulers of ancient Egypt were kings, whether they were men or women. There was only one ruler at a time. In ancient Egypt, if you were speaking to the king, you would say Pharaoh. If you were an ancient Egyptian, speaking about the current king, you would say Pharaoh. If speaking about a time in the past, whether you were an ancient Egyptian or a modern historian, you would refer to the pharaoh at that time by his or her name, preferenced by the word "king". Example: King Menes was a pharaoh of ancient Egypt.
-What are the three kingdoms of ancient Egypt?
Archaeologists divide ancient Egypt's history into three big blocks of time. In each block of time, all pharaohs behaved in a certain way. These blocks of time were called kingdoms. Scientists named these blocks the Old Kingdom, the Middle Kingdom, and the New Kingdom.
-What were laws like in ancient Egypt?
Laws were based on a common sense view of right and wrong, and on a belief of justice and fairness to all, as the ancient Egyptian goddess Ma'at, the goddess of truth and justice, would want. Most probably, some laws at least were written down, but so far, no remains of those writings have been found.
-What did the ancient Egyptians use for money?
They used a barter system, and also a system controlled by the government called metal weights.
-What is different about Egyptian architecture?
There are many unique features of Egyptian architecture They used ramps instead of stairs. Their houses were raised off the ground, they also built the first pyramids and obelisk.
-Why were Egyptian houses raised off the ground?
To keep out the sand. Egypt has deserts both east and west so sand was often in the air. To keep it out of the houses, they raised and sloped their floors.
-Why did the Egyptians use ramps instead of steps?
We don't really know.Two good possible explanations are, first ramps are easier to build than steps and second ramps are easier to keep clean of sand and dirt.
-Who built the first ancient Egyptian pyramid?
It was built by the vizier Imhotep for King Zoser.
-Were mummies really buried in pyramids?
Yes, the pyramids were tombs for kings, but pyramids fell out of style after a while, and kings (pharaohs) were buried in hidden tombs instead
-Were there really traps in the pyramids?
Yes. Since Egyptian kings were buried with their treasure, false passages and traps were set to keep out thieves.
-Who was King Tut and why is he important?
King Tut is short for King Tutankhamen. King Tut was only Pharaoh for about 9 years. Yet he is probably one of the most famous pharaohs of all. He was a teenager when he died. His people did not have time in nine years to built a huge tomb for him. They had to quickly finish a tiny tomb. Because his tomb was so small, archaeologists and grave robbers did not discover it until thousands of years later. It was not until 1922 that Howard Carter entered Tut's tomb and found a tomb full of treasures.
-What is an obelisk?
An obelisk is a tall, 4-sided structure, built like a column with a 4-sided pyramid on the top. The Egyptians covered these with paintings and hieroglyphics. Each obelisk told a story.
-What is the Great Sphinx?
The Great Sphinx is a stone monument built about 4000 years ago. It is located on the Giza Plateau in Egypt. It was carved out of limestone with a human face on a lion's body. The Sphinx was probably built to honor the Egyptian sun god, Re.
-What were the arts like in ancient Egypt?
The Egyptians were greats artists.They painted, sculpted and made great decorative jewelry.
-Did artists take credit for their work?
No, they did not.
-What was music like in ancient Egypt?
Egyptians loved music both instrumental and singing.They had flutes, harps, bells, lutes, drums, cymbals, rattles, and tambourines.
-Did ancient Egyptians dance?
Yes. Egyptians danced at festivals, holidays, and special occasions. Men danced with men and women danced with women, never men with women.
-Did the ancient Egyptians tell stories, myths, and legends?
They did. They created stories, myths, legends, poetry and even told a tall tale or two.
